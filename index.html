Our Story

From Tokyo to New York City

Opened in 1950, Sakura began as a beloved Japanese restaurant in Japan, known for its authentic flavors and dedication to quality. After years of delighting locals, the restaurant relocated to New York City, bringing its traditional recipes and unique culinary expertise to a new, vibrant audience. Today, Sakura continues to honor its Japanese roots while offering an unforgettable dining experience that blends the best of both worlds.

Special Dish

Sakura Hana Kaiseki

Sakura Hana Kaiseki is a multi-course dining experience that highlights rare, seasonal ingredients like A5 Wagyu, Uni, and Matsutake mushrooms, paired with locally sourced vegetables. The meal blends traditional Japanese techniques with subtle global influences, such as truffle-infused miso foam and lobster carpaccio, creating a unique and sophisticated culinary journey.
